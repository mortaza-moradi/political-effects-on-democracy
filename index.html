On American Liberalism and
Global Democracy

Here will go the abstract. It is a summary of the research inspiration, goals, and any methodology with a slight sneak peak into the findings.

It is often easy to dismiss the American partisan pendulum as a granted natural phenomenon; an endless oscillation that, afforded patience, favors the entire established political duality. Given these partisan swings and their destructive tendencies, only the hardiest policy recommendations and governmental adaptations seem guaranteed survival - with our democratic institutions assumed as the auxiliary beneficiaries. However, one overlooked aspect of America's political fluctuations is the international effect of conservative or liberal trends and the subsequent effect that transitional instability has on the global proliferation of democratic values.
